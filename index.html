About me

I am a theoretical astrophysicist working on the evolution, interactions, and mergers of massive stars across cosmic time. My research combines stellar evolution modelling and population synthesis to understand how binaries and mergers shape the brightest stars in galaxies, their chemical enrichment, and the progenitors of gravitational-wave sources.

I am currently based at Columbia University, where I balance an active research program with teaching in the Core “Frontiers of Science” course.
